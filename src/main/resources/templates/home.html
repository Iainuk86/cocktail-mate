Cocktail of the Month

Mai Tai

The Mai Tai started as a rum cocktail so popular it supposedly depleted world rum supplies in the 1940s and '50s. It was invented in 1944 by Victor J. Bergeron, better known as Trader Vic.

Bergeron highlighted the golden rum with lime, Orgeat, Orange Curaçao, and simple syrup. According to legend, after presenting the cocktail to some of his Tahitian friends, they exclaimed, "Maita’i roa a’e," which translates to "out of this world! The best!" Bergeron christened his new cocktail "Mai Tai", as in "the best".

Various juices were later added to appeal to tourists, the addition of which has unfortunately continued until today. Here we make it without.
